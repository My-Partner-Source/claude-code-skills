# LinkedIn Platform Guidelines

## Hard Constraints

- **Character limit**: 3,000 characters per post
- **"See more" fold**: ~210 characters before truncation
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 recommended (unlike X, these help discovery)
- **Links**: Can hurt reachâ€”consider putting in comments instead

## Platform Culture

LinkedIn rewards:
- Professional framing (but not corporate speak)
- Vulnerability about career struggles
- Concrete lessons from experience
- Content that makes readers look smart if they share it

LinkedIn penalizes:
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree")
- Controversial hot takes (unlike X)
- Overly casual tone
- Negativity without resolution

## Post Structure

### The Hook (First Line)

The first line before "See more" is everything. It must:
- Create curiosity or tension
- Be self-contained (make sense without the rest)
- Avoid starting with "I"

### Line Breaks Matter

LinkedIn's algorithm favors "dwell time" (how long people read). Use:
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Single sentences on their own lines for emphasis
- White space to create breathing room

## What NOT to Do

- **Engagement pods**: LinkedIn penalizes coordinated engagement
- **Comment bait**: "Comment 'GUIDE' to get my free PDF"
- **Negativity without insight**: Complaining without teaching
- **Humble brags**: "So humbled to announce..." (just announce it)
- **Walls of text**: No line breaks = no reads
- **Links in post body**: Put in comments to avoid reach penalty
- **Tagging people who didn't ask**: Feels spammy

## Link Strategy

LinkedIn deprioritizes posts with links (they want users to stay on platform).

**Options:**
1. Put link in first comment, mention "link in comments"
2. Use link only if essential to the post
3. For threads/articles, use LinkedIn's native article feature

## Posting Cadence

- 2-4 posts per week is sustainable
- Consistency beats frequency
- Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM (local timezone)
- Engage with comments for 30-60 min after posting (algorithm boost)

## Engagement Strategy

- Reply to every comment in first hour (signals engagement to algorithm)
- Ask follow-up questions to commenters
- Thank people for sharing (but don't overdo it)
- Cross-post wins from X as slightly longer LinkedIn versions
